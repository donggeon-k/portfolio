Machine Learning Guided Optimization for Extracellular Vesicle Production

I utilized predictive AI models to generate support vector machine plots that guided the optimization of extracellular vesicle production. We hypothesized that EV characterictics can be optimized for drug delivery by controlling culture parameters like temperature, oxygen saturation, pH level, and carbon source.

Quantifying Curcumin Loading Efficiencies and Release Profiles of L. Crispatus EVs

I investigated the loading efficiencies of curcumin into L. crispatus EVs and characterized their release profiles over time. This work aimed to establish a foundation for developing EV-based drug delivery systems. Here, I used electroporation, sonication, and isolation protocols to actively load curcumin into the EVs.

Quantifying Progesterone Loading Efficiencies and Release Profiles of L. Crispatus EVs

I expanded on the curcumin loading experiments by investigating the loading efficiencies of progesterone into L. crispatus EVs. This project aimed to characterize the release profiles of progesterone-loaded EVs over time, further establishing their potential as a drug delivery system. I isolated the progesterone-loaded EVs using high performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) to ensure purity and concentration for subsequent analyses.
